Types of Written Puzzle Games

Sudoku

Sudoku is a puzzle game you play on a peice of paper divided into a 9x9 square, which is then divided into 9 3x3 sections. You must make the numbers 1-9 fit in each row, column, and square without repeating.

Origin

The first modern game of Sudoku appeared in Japan in 1984. Sudoku puzzles gained popularity for their ease of understanding, ease of use when communiting, and because number puzzles work better in the Japanese language then words do. Sudoku was introduced to the Western world in March of 1997.

Crossword Puzzles

Crossword Puzzles are word games, usually in a square shaped grid full of black and white squares. You are given hints for down and across words. The answers are written down in the white spaces on the grid.

Origin

Crossword puzzles were first invented by British journalist Authur Wynne. They picked up popularity after being published in the New York Times during the early 1920s. From that point, crosswords evolved into other letter based games, such as the board game Scrabble and the online game "Words with Friends".

Coded Quotes

Coded Quotes are like ciphers. You are given a phrase in a scrambled letter format, and you need to assign the correct letters to break the code. So, the word "the" may be spelt "abc", which means t=a, h=b, and e=c.

Origins

Ciphers have been around for thousands of years, with the first documented one was created by the Greeks in 400 BCE, and were used to pass secret messages between military units and commanders.

Futoshiki

Futoshiki is a numbers and math game. A 5x5 grid is presented, with the mathematical operators < and > between some of the boxes on the grid. The goal is to get the numbers 1 through 5 in the grid, while matching the mathematical operators.

Origins

The game means "More or Less" in Japanese, and was developed by Tamaki Seto in 2001. Futoshiki is popular in the UK, and is published in many newspapers and magazines daily there.

Binario

Binario is a game where you try to get two symbols (generally 1 and 0) on the grid within certain specifications. Such as "no more than 3 0s and 3 1s per column and row".

Origins

There are 2 different rumored origins to the puzzle. One is Tohu wa Vohu (Tic Tac Logic), created by Adolfo Zanellati. The second rumored origin is for Binario as described above, which was created by Peter De Schepper and Frank Coussement in 2009.
